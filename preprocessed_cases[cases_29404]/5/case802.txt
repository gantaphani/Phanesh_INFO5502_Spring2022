tyler keup,an artist in the custody of the nebraska department of correctional services ndcs,tried to send drawings of a marijuana leaf and a woman to his mother and the maoist internationalist movement maoists.when ndcs rebuffed keup efforts,keup sued various ndcs officials under for violating his first amendment rights.the district court directed a verdict in keup favor against hopkins and dennis bakewell,awarded keup nominal damages,and ordered hopkins and bakewell to pay approximately in attorney fees and costs.hopkins and bakewell appeal.we affirm in part and reverse in part.
background 
keup is a prisoner in the custody of ndcs at the lincoln correctional center lcc.at all relevant times,hopkins was one of ndcs directors.bakewell was lcc warden.salvador cruz,ty dekoenig and diane were ndcs employees at lcc.
keup grievances 
while incarcerated,keup challenged various restrictions upon his ability to send and receive mail.keup availed himself of ndcs grievance procedure.to exhaust administrative remedies,a prisoner must file an informal grievance,a grievance,and a grievance.admin.code,ch.see also.
time limits apply at each level of the ndcs grievance process.a complaining inmate must file an informal grievance within three days of the incident of concern.if the inmate is dissatisfied with ndcs response,the inmate must file a grievance within fifteen days.if the inmate remains dissatisfied,the inmate must file a grievance within ten days of ndcs response to the grievance.
four incidents are relevant to this appeal 
march to mail drawings to mother 
in march,keup tried to send his mother two drawings,one of a cross containing a small marijuana leaf and the other a woman.at the time.of lcc operational memorandum om forbade inmates from manufacturing,possessing,or distributing drawings of u ncovered female breasts,illegal drugs,and any other obscene,nude,lewd,lascivious,or filthy material.lcc personnel accordingly refused to let keup mail the drawings.
keup timely grieved ndcs refusal,alleging a violation of his free speech rights.keup reasoned it seems illogical that i can receive explicit porn magazines and books containing images of drugs but i ca use my artistic abilities to create certain drawings for a good cause cruz denied keup informal grievance,bakewell denied keup grievance,and hopkins denied keup grievance.
september to mail photocopies to the maoists 
in september,keup tried to send photocopies of his drawings of the marijuana leaf and the woman to the maoists.apparently,keup was asking the maoists for legal help dekoenig intercepted the photocopies.
keup immediately grieved dekoenig seizure of the photocopies,asserting a violation of his right to send legal mail.on september,cruz denied keup informal grievance.on november,keup filed a grievance,reasserting his right to access for the conduction of legal affairs.on december,bakewell denied the grievance as untimely.on july,keup filed a grievance,in which he repeated the claims in his and informal grievances.keup alleged he was unable to file his grievance in a timely manner because lcc personnel segregated him from the general prison population on september.on august,hopkins denied keup grievance as untimely and pointed out segregated prisoners have unfettered access to ndcs grievance procedure.
december to receive pastor sukraw card 
in late december,pastor joe sukraw,a religious cleric from north platte,nebraska,purchased a religious book and a greeting card for keup.the bookstore sent keup the book and the card in a single mailing,and lcc personnel intercepted the shipment.section of the om provided that a ll publications must be prepaid and sent to the inmate directly from the publisher but cards can not be included in mailings from the.lcc personnel returned the card to the sender,stapled a return notice to the book,and delivered the book to keup.
keup timely grieved the return of the card,claiming lcc personnel violated the om.keup complained the stapling of the return notice to the book was highly disrespectful,degrading,and equivical sic to leaving shoe prints all over ones sic mail before delivery.keup asked for a new book,because the first fourteen pages of his book had staple holes in them.cruz denied keup informal grievance,denied keup grievance,and hopkins denied keup grievance.
february to receive a bondage sketchbook 
in february,lcc personnel intercepted a bondage sketchbook in keup mail,because the om forbade prisoners from possessing depictions of violent sexual acts.keup timely grieved the interception of the bondage sketchbook,opining that bondage is a sexual fetish and is not of a violent nature or an illegal nature and querying how an art book containing various sketches of nude women could pose a threat cruz denied keup informal grievance,denied keup grievance,and hopkins denied keup grievance.
prior proceedings 
in august,keup filed a complaint against hopkins,bakewell,and cruz under.keup claimed hopkins,bakewell,and cruz violated keup first and fourteenth amendment rights when they prohibited keup from sending his drawings of marijuana and the woman to his mother.keup requested compensatory and punitive damages,as well as declaratory and injunctive relief.
in may,hopkins,bakewell,and cruz answered the complaint.they denied the complaint substance and asserted keup request for an injunction is moot since changes have been made in the om that allow the plaintiff to do what he seeks the injunction to do.in september,ndcs amended the om to ban only drawings that advocate or are likely to incite violent or illegal activity.
amended complaint 
in august,keup amended his complaint with leave of court.in addition to the claims in the complaint,keup lodged additional first and fourteenth amendment claims under against hopkins,bakewell,dekoenig,cruz,and for their respective roles in blocking the mailing of the photocopies to the maoists seizing the greeting card and the bondage sketchbook and allegedly retaliating against keup for filing the complaint collectively,the new claims.keup again requested monetary,declaratory,and injunctive relief.
in august and september,hopkins,bakewell,dekoenig,cruz,and answered the amended complaint.these defendants variously denied the substance of the amended complaint,asserted keup claims were moot to the extent keup was asking for permission to send his drawings to his mother and the maoists,and maintained keup failed to exhaust his administrative remedies as to his new claims.
motion for summary judgment 
in november,hopkins,bakewell,dekoenig,cruz,and moved for summary judgment,asserting their exhaustion and mootness defenses.in february,the district court rejected the defendants exhaustion defenses because,in its view,dismissal of keup new claims would result in another lawsuit and foster judicial inefficiency.the district court rejected the defendants mootness defenses as to keup request for monetary damages,but held keup requests for prospective injunctive and declaratory relief were moot because ndcs had amended the om.
jury trial 
in july,the district court held a jury trial on keup requests for damages.at the conclusion of keup,the district court directed a verdict in favor of keup and against hopkins and bakewell for prohibit ing the plaintiff from mailing his drawings to his mother and the maoists.the district court awarded keup in nominal damages and invited an application for attorney fees.all of keup remaining claims failed when the district court directed a verdict in the defendants favor as to some claims and the jury rejected all others.
