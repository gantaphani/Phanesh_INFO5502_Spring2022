ramon alfonso zanoletti alfonso and his wife magdalena rosalis zanoletti magdalena appeal from the judgments entered upon their convictions by jury of insurance fraud.specifically,alfonso was convicted of counts of felony insurance fraud,subd.a counts,with findings of a pattern of fraudulent taking of more than,subd.a and damages exceeding,subd.a.he was also convicted of one count count of misdemeanor unauthorized practice of law bus,subd.a.magdalena was convicted of the same insurance fraud counts and special allegations,plus another counts of felony insurance fraud,subd.a counts,with the same special allegations.
the trial court denied probation and sentenced each appellant to years in state prison calculated as follows as to count,the midterm of three years as to the remaining counts under section,subdivision a,one year or the midterm to be served consecutively plus one year on the section,subdivision a allegation.pursuant to section,the court stayed the sentence on count unauthorized practice of law,and stayed the sentences on the additional counts of insurance fraud under section,subdivision a,striking the special allegations as to these counts.
the trial court also ordered each appellant to pay a restitution fine,subd.b,a dna penalty assessment,a court security fee,subd.a,a aggravated white collar crime fine,subd.c,and in victim restitution to the defrauded insurance companies,subd.f,and imposed and suspended a parole revocation fine.appellants each received days of custody credit.
on appeal,appellants each raise numerous contentions,including instructional errors,sufficiency of the evidence,expiration of a statute of limitations and sentencing errors.magdalena also contends that she could not have been convicted of violating more than one subdivision of section and that her convictions of violating both subdivisions a and a run afoul of the doctrine.we disagree and publish this portion of our opinion.although we find most of appellants remaining contentions to be without merit in the unpublished portion of our opinion,we remand the matter for determination as to whether prosecution should be tolled on count against alfonso and for determination of the appropriate amount of the dna identification fund penalty assessment.we also modify the judgments on our findings of sentencing errors.in all other respects,the judgments are affirmed.

prosecution case background 
magdalena worked as an office manager at the franklin chiropractic clinic on west street in los angeles the clinic and received an annual salary of.clarence franklin worked as a chiropractor at the clinic.magdalena husband alfonso leased the space for the clinic and also leased some of the medical equipment,including the machine,and paid the clinic telephone bill.alfonso,who was not an attorney,had an office at the law offices of taghizadeh associates the law office.between december,and december,alfonso received from the law firm in sporadic checks of unequal sizes,often with the notation for.appellants filed joint tax returns.
on march,law enforcement arrested constantino pineda,a capper who specialized in organizing and staging car accidents for the purpose of engaging in insurance fraud.pineda was in possession of a capping book that listed names,including alfonso,dates and other information pertaining to accidents he had organized.also in his possession were business cards for alfonso and the clinic.based largely on information obtained from pineda,search warrants were executed at the clinic and the law office.various documents were seized from the clinic,including a capping book listing patients who corresponded to those in pineda capping book.
california highway patrol sergeant tannon brown,an expert on automobile insurance fraud,led a investigation on insurance claims arising from patients listed in the clinic capping book.the investigation focused on patients,as discussed below,and the period between january and december.sergeant brown opined that the clinic was an insurance fraud mill,that alfonso was a capper who specialized in staging accidents and then referring people to both the clinic and the law office,and that magdalena prepared fraudulent paperwork that was provided to the law office,which then submitted the paperwork to insurance companies in support of demands for payment.

discussion 
convictions of both section,subdivisions a and a.
insurance fraud is proscribed by section,subdivision a,which includes nine enumerated acts that constitute violations each section,subdivision a count charged against appellants related to an incident involving a specific individual there was a corresponding subdivision a charge for the same incident.for the first time on appeal,magdalena contends that she could not have been convicted of violating both subdivisions of section,reasoning that the different subdivisions merely describe different means of committing the single offense of insurance fraud.she argues that because her conduct was more completely covered by section,subdivision a,she should not have been convicted of also violating section,subdivision a.alternatively,she contends that her convictions of violating both subdivisions run afoul of the doctrine.
multiple convictions were appropriate 
the people agree that the various paragraphs of section,subdivision a describe different means of committing the single crime of insurance fraud and that a defendant can not be convicted of multiple violations of section,subdivision a for the same act.for example,the people note that the conduct described in subdivision a seems to be entirely encompassed by subdivision a.but they argue that magdalena multiple convictions were appropriate because they were based on different acts of fraud.we agree
