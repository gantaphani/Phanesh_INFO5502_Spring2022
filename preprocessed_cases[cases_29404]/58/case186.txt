where a plaintiff brings a lawsuit against a defendant,and the trial court denies a motion by the defendant for summary judgment or for nonsuit made after the plaintiff,or the trier of fact returns a verdict for the plaintiff,that ruling or verdict if decided on the merits and not procured by fraud establishes as a matter of law that the plaintiff had probable cause to bring its lawsuit and precludes a subsequent claim against the plaintiff for maliciously prosecuting that lawsuit,even if the trier of fact later rules for the defendant or the verdict is later overturned.wilson parker,covert chidester cal th,wilson.does this rule apply and is a subsequent malicious prosecution claim barred when the trial court in the prior lawsuit denied the defendant motion for judgment under code of civil procedure section but went on to rule in the defendant favor we conclude that it does.accordingly,we affirm the trial court judgment dismissing this malicious prosecution action.

in,defendant gingko rose gingko rose acquired a house occupied by six tenants,including plaintiffs wayne hart hart and carlos rodriguez rodriguez.defendants david and barbara darwish own part of gingko rose,and defendant logerm llc is gingko rose general partner.defendants lisa rosenthal and her law firm,rosenthal associates,represented gingko rose.

ii.procedural background 
all six tenants sued gingko rose,the darwishes,logerm llc,rosenthal and her law firm collectively,defendants for malicious prosecution of the unlawful detainer lawsuit.they sought compensatory damages,emotional distress damages,and punitive damages.
defendants moved for judgment on the pleadings on the ground that the unlawful detainer court denial of the motion for judgment in hart and rodriguez trial mandated a finding that defendants had probable cause to bring the unlawful detainer lawsuit,thereby dictating dismissal of the malicious prosecution action.
the trial court granted the motion as to hart and rodriguez.the court found that the unlawful detainer court had weighed the evidence and denied the section motion,which in its view dictated a finding that the evidence for defendants unlawful detainer claim must at least be tenable under the lenient standard for evaluating probable cause to bring and maintain a lawsuit.the court rejected the tenants argument that the unlawful detainer court ruling was a product of defendants fraud,finding no evidence to back up that argument.the court accordingly granted defendants judgment on the pleadings as to hart and rodriguez,the only two tenants before the unlawful detainer court when it denied the section motion.the trial court later denied hart and rodriguez motion for a new trial.
hart and rodriguez filed timely notices of appeal.
