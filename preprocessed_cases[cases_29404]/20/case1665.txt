mark vroman was convicted on one count of murder with a firearm specification in ohio state court.vroman appeals the district court dismissal of his petition for a writ of habeas corpus as under the applicable statute of limitations set forth in the antiterrorism and effective death penalty act of aedpa,d.for the following reasons,we affirm the judgment of the district court.
on july,an ohio jury convicted vroman of murder with a firearm specification.he was sentenced to a term of fifteen years to life in prison for the murder conviction and a term of three years for the use of the firearm to be served consecutively with and prior to the murder sentence.vroman appealed his conviction to the ohio court of appeals,and on june,the court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.on december,the ohio supreme court denied vroman request for leave to appeal and dismissed the appeal as not involving any substantial constitutional question.
on august,vroman filed a delayed application for reopening his appeal pursuant to ohio appellate rule b,and the application was denied as untimely.vroman then filed a pro se appeal of the ohio court of appeals decision with the ohio supreme court on october.the ohio supreme court dismissed vroman appeal.
prior to the above proceedings,on or about september,vroman filed a delayed petition to vacate or set aside his sentence.the trial court denied this action as untimely on november.vroman appealed and on april,the ohio court of appeals affirmed the trial court judgment.on september,the ohio supreme court dismissed vroman subsequent appeal.finally,on november,vroman filed a motion for relief from judgment requesting that the trial court vacate its november,decision dismissing his action as untimely filed.on december,the trial court denied vroman motion.on december,the ohio court of appeals affirmed the judgment of the trial court.the ohio supreme court declined to hear the case and dismissed vroman appeal on april.
on november,vroman,acting pro se,filed a petition for writ of habeas corpus pursuant to in the united states district court for the southern district of ohio.in his habeas petition,vroman alleged sixteen grounds for relief.the warden,anthony brigano,moved to dismiss the petition for failing to comply with the statute of limitations imposed by d.on march,the district court denied the warden motion to dismiss,concluding that vroman untimely ohio petition tolled the running of the statute of limitations under d.the warden moved for reconsideration and the district court denied the motion.thereafter,the warden filed a return of writ,and on december,the district court dismissed the petition as due to the sixth circuit decision in raglin randle,wl cir.may.vroman moved for reconsideration,and on january,the district court denied his motion.
on february,vroman filed a notice of appeal and an application for a certificate of appealability.the district court granted a certificate of appealability on the issues of whether the statute of limitations expired prior to filing the instant petition and whether vroman is entitled to an evidentiary hearing to present evidence that his action was timely filed in state court.
the dismissal of a habeas petition by the district court as barred by statute of limitations is reviewed de novo.cook stegall,cir,cert.denied,d.where the facts of the case are undisputed or the district court rules as a matter of law that equitable tolling is unavailable,this court reviews the decision de novo.dunlap united states,cir.
under aedpa,a prisoner has one year from the completion of the direct review of his case to commence a collateral attack on his conviction.d.this one year limitation period runs from the latest of 
a the date on which the judgment became final by the conclusion of direct review or the expiration of the time for seeking such review 
b the date on which the impediment to filing an application created by state action in violation of the constitution or laws of the united states is removed,if the applicant was prevented from filing by such state action 
c the date on which the constitutional right asserted was initially recognized by the supreme court,if the right has been newly recognized by the supreme court and made retroactively applicable to cases on collateral review or 
d the date on which the factual predicate of the claim or claims presented could have been discovered through the exercise of due diligence.
id.vroman conviction became final on march,ninety days after the ohio supreme court dismissed his direct appeal.see bronaugh ohio,cir.therefore,in the absence of any tolling,vroman had until march,to file his habeas petition.
the aedpa limitations period may be tolled for that period of time during which a properly filed application for state relief or other collateral review with respect to the pertinent judgment or claim is pending.d emphasis added.the tolling provision does not,however,revive the limitations period,restart the clock at zero it can only serve to pause a clock that has not yet fully run.once the limitations period is expired,collateral petitions can no longer serve to avoid a statute of limitations.rashid khulmann.
on august,during the relevant period,vroman filed a delayed ohio appellate rule b action and a petition.the petition was ultimately dismissed by the ohio supreme court on january.because vroman filed the rule b action days after his conviction became final,vroman had days remaining of his aedpa statute of limitations as of january.therefore,vroman must also receive tolling during the time his action remained pending in the state courts to prevent his habeas petition,filed on november,from being time barred.
effective september,ohio amended its statute to include a time limitation period of days from the date the criminal trial transcript is filed in the appellate court for direct review,subject to several exceptions ohio revised code a.cases in which a sentence was imposed prior to september,were given a grace period,and,therefore,vroman deadline for filing any petition seeking relief was september.ohio revised code a.because that particular date fell on a saturday,the actual deadline was monday,september.ohio criminal rule a.
according to the ohio trial court,vroman petition was filed on september,one day late.the ohio trial court concluded that vroman petition was not timely filed according to ohio law.vroman appealed,arguing that his petition was timely filed because he delivered the petition to prison authorities for mailing on september,within the applicable time limit the mailbox rule.the appellate court rejected vroman argument,holding that a filing is timely only if it is filed with the court within the appropriate time limits the jailer does not represent the court for filing purposes.
vroman then moved for relief from judgment pursuant to ohio civil rule b,arguing that the clerk of court actually received his petition on september,and,therefore,his petition was timely.in support of his argument,vroman submitted two letters he wrote to the postmaster of the post office that delivers mail to the ross county clerk of courts.the postmaster made handwritten notes on the letters stating that mail is delivered to the courthouse at on a daily basis,and that mail is never delivered prior to vroman petition was stamped received on september,at therefore,vroman claimed that his petition was received by the clerk on september,but was not stamped until september.the ohio state courts rejected vroman argument.
