opinion of the court 
medical residencies are a vital component of american medical education.mckeesport hosp.acgme,cir.they provide new doctors a supervised transition between the pure academics of medical school and the realities of practice.generally they do so successfully our nation residency programs reliably produce some of the finest physicians and medical researchers in the world.a a.but as this case shows,these programs are exempt from charges of sex discrimination.here we must decide whether an,proceeding anonymously as jane doe,can bring private causes of action for sex discrimination under title ix of the education amendments of,et,against mercy catholic medical center,a private teaching hospital operating a residency program.the district court held she can not and dismissed her complaint in its entirety.we will affirm in part and reverse in part that order.doe title ix retaliation and quid pro quo claims endure.her title ix hostile environment claim is,however.
we recount the facts as doe alleged them,accepting them as true.davis wells fargo,cir.see app.
graduate medical education,or residency education,is a period of didactic and clinical instruction in a medical specialty during which physicians prepare for independent practice after graduating from medical school.residency programs are typically accredited.leading on that front is the accreditation council for graduate medical education,or acgme,which aims to improve healthcare by assessing and advancing the quality of residents educations.its reach is far and its influence wide.during the academic year,around programs operated in about institutions,enrolling over residents and fellows in medical specialties.the acgme calls these programs structured educational experiences,and completing one generally results in eligibility for board certification.
predictably,residency programs are expensive to run.the association of american medical colleges says it costs a hospital about a year to train a single resident.but the federal government helps with funding by way of direct and indirect graduate medical education payments through medicare.
our case is about a residency program at mercy,a private teaching hospital in philadelphia that accepts medicare payments and is affiliated with drexel university college of medicine.owing to its commitment to medical education,mercy offers four residency programs in internal medicine,diagnostic radiology,general surgery,and a transitional year residency,in addition to providing the clinical bases for drexel medicine emergency medicine residency.
under a residency agreement,doe joined mercy diagnostic radiology residency program in as a,or.the program offered training in all radiology subspecialties in a setting combining experience with didactic teaching.as required,doe attended daily morning lectures presented by faculty and afternoon case presentations given by residents under faculty or attending physicians supervision.she took a mandatory physics class taught on drexel campus,attended monthly radiology lectures and society meetings,joined in interdepartmental conferences,and sat for annual examinations to assess her progress and competence.
doe says the director of mercy residency program,whom she calls james roe,sexually harassed her and retaliated against her for complaining about his behavior,resulting in her eventual dismissal.early on,roe inquired about her personal life and learned she was living apart from her husband.he found opportunities to see and speak with her more than would otherwise be expected,often looking at her suggestively.this made doe uncomfortable,especially when the two were alone.from these interactions she surmised roe was sexually attracted to her and wished to pursue a relationship,though they both were married.
three months into her residency doe sent roe an email voicing concern that others knew about his interest in her.she wanted their relationship to remain professional,she said,but roe persisted,stating he wanted to meet with her while they attended a conference in chicago.she replied with text messages to clear the air that she did want to pursue a relationship with him.apparently displeased,roe reported these messages to mercy human resources department,or hr.in response,hr called doe to a meeting where she described roe conduct,like how he touched her hand at work,and said his unwelcome sexual attention was negatively affecting her training.the next day hr referred doe to a psychiatrist,noting that her attendance was optional.doe,however,believed mercy would use it against her if she did go,given her complaints against roe.she thus attended three sessions and complained there about roe conduct,but she heard nothing more from hr.later roe apologized to doe for reporting her.he did it,he said,for fear he be reprimanded for having an inappropriate relationship with her.thereafter two male faculty members,both close with roe,trained her significantly less than they had before.
in fall roe learned doe was getting divorced.his overtures intensified.he too was getting divorced,he told her,and he wanted a relationship with her.he suggested they go shooting and travel together.he said he was uncomfortable with her going to dinner for fellowship interviews and unhappy about her leaving philadelphia.during this time doe asked roe and another faculty member for fellowship recommendation letters.they agreed but wrote short,cursory,and perfunctory ones.roe even told the fellowship director that doe was a poor candidate.when doe called roe to ask why,he said it was to teach her a lesson before hanging up on her.
in response to doe complaints about roe,mercy vice president,arnold eiser,called doe to a meeting with roe and others.there doe complained about roe conduct again but was told to wait outside.a short time later eiser escorted her to mercy psychiatrist.as they walked eiser told doe her second examination score was poor,an issue she needed to address.later,however,doe learned this was true her score was in the percentile,and eiser had received misinformation.she asked roe to report her improvement to the fellowship she applied to,but he refused.mercy later told doe that to remain in the program,she have to agree to a corrective plan.reluctantly,she signed on.
roe conduct continued into spring.once while doe was sitting alone with roe at a computer reviewing radiology reports,he reached across her body and placed his hand on hers to control the mouse,pressing his arm against her breasts in the process.she pushed herself back in her chair,stood up,and protested.another time,when a physician expressed interest in doe,roe became jealous and told doe she should date him.later,in april roe told another resident to remove doe name as coauthor from a research paper she contributed to.doe complained,but roe said she was acting unprofessionally and ordered her to another meeting with eiser.at that meeting doe again told eiser about roe conduct over the past year.eiser,however,said the other residents loved roe and told her to apologize to him.she did,but roe would accept it,calling it insincere.eiser suspended doe,recommending another visit to the psychiatrist.
thereafter on april,doe received a letter from mercy stating she been terminated but could appeal.she appeared before an appeals committee four days later where she described roe behavior.roe appeared there too advocating for her dismissal.he did so,she says,because she rejected his advances.the committee upheld doe dismissal,giving her five days to bring another appeal.she declined and quit the program,with mercy accepting her resignation.since then,no other residency program has accepted her,blocking her from full licensure.
doe sued mercy in the district court on april,exactly two years after she learned she been dismissed.seeking damages and equitable relief,she alleges six claims,three under title ix retaliation,quid pro quo,and hostile environment and three under pennsylvania law sex discrimination,wrongful termination,and breach of the covenant of good faith and fair dealing.she concedes she never filed a charge with the equal employment opportunity commission,or eeoc,under title vii of the civil rights act of,et.
ultimately the district court dismissed the third iteration of doe complaint under federal rule of civil procedure b.title ix does apply to mercy,the court held,because it not an education program or activity under a.even if title ix did apply,it stated,doe ca use title ix to circumvent title vii administrative requirements,as congress intended title vii as the exclusive avenue for relief for employment discrimination.supp.the court also found doe hostile environment claim untimely.having dismissed all doe title ix claims,the court declined jurisdiction of her state law claims.doe timely appealed.
the district court had jurisdiction under and a,and we have it under.we exercise plenary review of a rule b dismissal,in re asbestos products liability litigation no.vi,cir,affirming if the plaintiff failed to allege plausible claims,see ashcroft iqbal.
our analysis is threefold.we address whether title ix applies to mercy,whether doe private causes of action are cognizable under title ix,and what to do about doe state law claims.title ix applicability to mercy is first.
we start,of course,with title ix language,north haven board of education bell,which says,no person in the united states shall,on the basis of sex,be excluded from participation in,be denied the benefits of,or be subjected to discrimination under any education program or activity receiving federal financial assistance,a emphasis added.we must decide,then,if mercy operation of a residency program makes it an education program or activity under title ix.
we note this question of first impression reaches far beyond one private lawsuit.it touches on the executive very power to address gender discrimination in residency programs under existing federal law.congress enacted title ix under its spending clause powers,making it in the nature of a contract in accepting federal funds,states agree to comply with its mandate.jackson birmingham bd.of.given its origins,title ix only express enforcement mechanism is through agencies regulation of federal funding.congress directs agencies to effectuate a by,among other means,the termination of or refusal to grant or to continue funding to education programs.see fitzgerald barnstable sch.today this directive applies afar federal agencies currently enforce title ix.see nondiscrimination on the basis of sex in education programs or activities receiving federal financial assistance,fed.reg,hereinafter title ix common rule codified in various sections of the code of federal regulations.and no other federal statute empowers agencies to restrict funding from education programs engaging in sex discrimination.title vi bars only race,color,and national origin discrimination,not sex discrimination.see gebser lago vista indep.sch.title vii is rooted in the commerce clause and of the fourteenth amendment,not the spending clause.see regents of of cal.bakke,brennan,white,marshall,blackmun,concurring in the judgment in part and dissenting in part gebser,at.and only a citizen of the united states or person within the jurisdiction thereof can sue under for unconstitutional sex discrimination in education programs.see fitzgerald,at.mindful of title ix place in this intricate scheme,we tread carefully.
to resolve whether mercy residency program makes it an education program or activity,we must square title ix definition of a program or activity,codified at,with a language education program or activity.this requires a brief look at title ix history.
patterned after title vi,title ix was enacted through the education amendments of in which congress set out a education program or activity language.in grove city college bell,however,the supreme court read that phrase narrowly,holding that the receipt of federal funds by a particular program within an institution does not trigger institutionwide coverage under title ix.congress disagreed.overruling grove city college it passed the civil rights restoration act of,or crra,to define the phrase program or activity broadly in provisions of four civil rights statutes title vi,the rehabilitation act,b the age discrimination in employment act,and title ix.see ncaa smith.
as amended by the crra,title ix now says in that program or activity means all of the operations of the following kinds of entities,any part of which is extended federal funding 
state or local government instrumentalities,
colleges,universities,postsecondary institutions,public systems of higher education,local educational agencies,vocational education systems,and other school systems,id.
entire corporations,partnerships,other private organizations,and sole proprietorships if assistance is extended to them as a whole or they principally engaged in the business of providing education,health care,housing,social services,or parks and recreation,id.a 
entire plants or other comparable,geographically separate facilities in the case of any other corporation,partnership,private organization,or sole proprietorship not described in subsection a,id.b and 
any other entity established by two or more entities described in subsections through,id.
in enacting,however,congress retained in a the modifier education before program or activity.it left education undefined and gave no guidance to reconcile broad phrase program or activity with a ostensibly narrower language.case law is scant on the issue.the supreme court has never addressed it.nor have we.down this unmarked path we must now travel.
how did the district court navigate it it focused on the fact that in enacting the crra,congress kept the word education in a.that,combined with c which defines an educational institution in part as any public or private preschool,elementary,or secondary school,or any institution of vocational,professional,or higher education clearly contemplated cabining title ix to education programs in the sense of schooling.supp.at.title ix thus could apply to mercy,it held,as residents already have a degree,do pay tuition,and are paid for their services and protected by labor laws.
respectfully,we find this approach wanting.sections a and extend title ix to education programs or activities,not to the educational institutions of c.where congress used specific language in one part of a statute but different language in another,we presume different meanings were intended.sosa.that especially so here,where congress used educational institution only in provisions to describe where title ix does control.see a,b,jackson,at section a subsections are specific,narrow exceptions to title ix.north haven,at same.we also query if congress intended to limit education programs or activities only to educational institutions in the sense of schooling,why did it enact detailed provisions expressly exempting noneducational institutions like social fraternities,the ymca,and the girl scouts from title ix reach see,a.those organizations would have already been impliedly exempt from title ix,rendering superfluous a express exemptions for them.because we strive to avoid superfluity in construing statutes,see corley united states,we reject this reading of title ix.
what direction does mercy suggest we take tacitly conceding that c is the way,they abandon it for a ii.that provision says program or activity means all the operations of a private entity principally engaged in the business of providing education,health care,housing,social services,or parks and recreation.but because a says education program or activity,mercy tells us we to ignore the words health care,housing,social services,or parks and recreation and hold that title ix applies only to private entities principally engaged in the business of providing education.applying that reading,mercy deems the result inevitable a private hospital like mercy that employs physicians in its own residency program is quite plainly not principally engaged in the education business.mercy br.
if only it were so plain.yet no part of title ix says it reaches only entities principally engaged in the business of providing education.quite the opposite.section leaves space aplenty for a variety of entities irrespective of what they principally engaged in for example,state and local government instrumentalities,private entities extended assistance as a whole,other private entities entire plants or separate facilities,and any entity established by two or more covered entities.more important,mercy approach strikes out considerable portions of a ii text.doe helpful visual aid puts that much on display mercy suggests title ix applies only to private entities principally engaged in the business of providing education,health care,housing,social services,or parks and recreation.reply br.strikethrough in original.by that reading we can not abide,for it violates a most basic interpretive rule that a statute is to be construed so that effect is given to all its provisions,so no part will be inoperative or superfluous,void or insignificant.corley,at.
it is then doe who,we think,charts the soundest course.she says,and we agree,there no reason to read the phrase education program or activity so narrowly.the supreme court has twice instructed us that,to give title ix the scope its origins dictate,we to accord it a sweep as broad as its language.north haven,at see jackson,at.and indeed the ordinary meaning of education a word congress has yet to define is very broad.roubideaux north dakota dep t of corrs,cir.congress expressly exempted specific kinds of programs from title ix reach like military academies,religious schools,and sororities,see a so we hesitant to impose further restrictions without strong justifications from title ix text.see north haven,at the absence of a specific proffered exclusion from a exceptions tends to support that it should be inferred.jeldness pearce,cir.because a lists specific exemptions,others are not to be judicially implied.the statute offers no such justification,so we reconcile with a as follows.
like the second circuit we hold that a program or activity under is an education program or activity under a if it has features such that one could reasonably consider its mission to be,at least in part,educational.davis,cir.this accords with title ix text and structure.it lines up with the eighth and ninth circuits applications of title ix beyond educational institutions in the sense of schooling to entire systems offering inmates educational programs.see klinger dep t of,cir.roubideaux,at jeldness,at.it consistent with the first circuit application of title ix to a university medical residency program.see lipsett of puerto rico,cir.and it in step with how federal agencies,including the departments of education and health and human services,have interpreted the statute.see title ix common rule,supra,at all saying title ix applies to any education program or activity whether or not it offered or sponsored by an educational institution amicus br.we adopt it.
we recognize,however,that creative minds could conceivably read the word education in title ix to encompass every experience of life,roubideaux,at,transforming title ix into a remedy for any dispute in which someone is potentially learning something,doe,supp.at.we see no sign congress intended as much.indeed by merely including the word education in a,congress signified that title ix has some boundary.we endeavor here to delimit it.
we note first that title ix application turns primarily on whether the questioned program or activity has educational characteristics.the plaintiff characteristics for example,whether she a student,employee,or something else may be relevant in some cases,but they are necessarily dispositive.that caveat aside,we highlight here several features that support deeming a program or activity an education program or activity under title ix,emphasizing that particular features or other features not here listed may be more or less relevant depending on the unique circumstances of each case.in no particular order,these features are that a a program is incrementally structured through a particular course of study or training,whether full or b a program allows participants to earn a degree or diploma,qualify for a certification or certification examination,or pursue a specific occupation or trade beyond mere training c a program provides instructors,examinations,an evaluation process or grades,or accepts tuition or d the entities offering,accrediting,or otherwise regulating a program hold it out as educational in nature.accord,at education programs typically provide instructors,evaluations,and offer a particular course of training.these guidelines are,we think,in keeping with the common understanding of the word education prevalent when title ix was enacted.see,webster new world dictionary ed.education is the process of training and developing the knowledge,skill,mind,character,esp.by formal schooling teaching training.taniguchi kan pac.saipan,when a term goes undefined in a statute,we give it its ordinary meaning.
we end with this whether a program or activity is sufficiently educational under title ix is a mixed question of law and fact.when the facts are uncontested,the judge decides the matter.factual disputes material to her legal conclusion are,however,left for the finder of fact.
applying this reading,we identify two plausible ways mercy residency program makes it an education program or activity under title ix.

we hasten to note,however,that our assessment of the educational features of mercy residency program does not imply that one must perform a analysis on each and every prerequisite to title ix coverage.for instance,whether a covered program or activity receives federal financial assistance,a,is determined by reference to the entire entity or whole organization,id.congress made that clear in overruling grove city college.see,at.with respect to federal financial assistance for purposes of title ix coverage,our analysis here does not alter the requirement of an assessment.
second we find it plausible mercy operation of a residency program makes its mission,at least in part,educational under title ix because of mercy affiliat ion with drexel medicine,app,a university program plausibly covered by title ix,see a.two decisions guide us lam curators of umkc dental school,cir,and from the second circuit.
in lam a clinician hired a university dental student to work at his private office un affiliated with the university.at.alleging the clinician sexually assaulted her there,the student sued the university under title ix.the university argued that she failed to show a nexus between the private office and the university,id.at,and the eighth circuit agreed,holding that the independent,private dental practice was a program or activity of the university under title ix.id.an education program,the court explained,is one controlled by and that inures some benefit to the covered institution.id.in the student case,it found,the clinician conferred no benefit to the university by operating a separate,competing clinic,as the university exercised no control over it and did provide it staff,funding,or any other support.id.
similarly in a college arranged for its student to serve as an unpaid intern at a hospital.at.alleging she was sexually harassed there,the intern sued the college and hospital under title ix,but the college was dismissed from the case.the intern argued that title ix reached the hospital because it accepted interns and thus operated a vocational training program.id.at.framing the issue as whether title ix applied to a hospital that allowed students to volunteer from a college with which it had no affiliation,the second circuit disagreed.id.at.the hospital,it found,maintained none of the characteristics associated with being an educator,unlike,for example,a teaching hospital mixed context.id.at quoting lipsett,at.and the college status as an education program could be imputed to the hospital,it held,because there was no evidence of an institutional affiliation,a written agreement binding them,shared staff,or funds circulated between them.id.
our case is different.unlike lam where the private dental office was un affiliated with the university,at,here we accept as true that mercy residency program is affiliated with drexel medicine,app.doe supports that contention with allegations that she took a physics class taught on drexel campus,app,and that mercy provided the clinical bases for drexel medicine emergency medicine residency,app.it thus plausible,we think,that mercy residency program inured some benefit to drexel medicine and vice versa and that these entities shared staff,funding,and other support.lam,at see iqbal,at rule inquiry is a task requiring us to draw on our judicial experience and common sense.
is distinguishable too.there the hospital accepted from a college with which it had no institutional affiliation.at.here,in contrast,doe expressly alleges such an affiliation between mercy and drexel medicine.and given her supporting allegations,we find it plausible to infer an agreement binding them and the sharing of staff and funds.id.given these alleged connections,it plausible mercy operation of a residency program affiliated with drexel medicine makes its mission,at least in part,educational under title ix,satisfying a.we will therefore vacate the district court order so far as it concludes otherwise.
of our first inquiry just one matter remains.in a lengthy footnote mercy claims it does receive federal financial assistance under title ix because its medicare payments stem from contracts of insurance.mercy br.mercy,however,made no such argument in the district court.our rule is well established in that circumstance theories not raised squarely there can not be surfaced for the first time on appeal.lesende borrero,cir.see united states joseph,cir.seeing no reason to depart from this rule mercy offers none,we decline to consider this argument,particularly as contracts of insurance in federal civil rights statutes intend to refer to contracts in the traditional sense,like those involving individual bank accounts in a bank with federally guaranteed deposits.united states baylor.med,cir.we thus assume without deciding that mercy receives federal financial assistance under title ix,leaving it for the district court to address on remand.
we continue to our second inquiry whether doe private causes of action are cognizable under title ix.as we said above,title ix provides just one express enforcement mechanism action through federal agencies.see.but in cannon university of chicago the supreme court held that title ix implies a cause of action for private litigants.we must decide,therefore,if cannon extends to doe title ix retaliation,quid pro quo,and hostile environment claims.
mercy says,and the district court agreed,a roadblock stands in doe way title vii.residents are employees,mercy submits,and title vii governs employment relationships,prohibiting discrimination based on sex.see a,a covington int l of approved basketball officials,cir.but,mercy notes,title vii also sets out elaborate administrative requirements an employee must satisfy before seeking relief in court.see burgh borough council of borough of montrose,cir.title ix is,in contrast,bare.while it requires proof an appropriate person had notice of the alleged discrimination so the institution had an opportunity to address it,see jackson,at gebser,at,title ix does have administrative hurdles like title vii.this means title ix plaintiffs can file directly in court under cannon implied cause of action.fitzgerald,at.given title vii framework,mercy contends,the district court was right that congress intended title vii as the sole avenue of private relief for employees of education programs who allege sex discrimination.private title ix claims alleging the same conduct,mercy argues,are not cognizable because they allow employees to plead their way round title vii administrative scheme.
we agree with just one part of this assessment.while we wo and ca speak for all residents,we agree here it plausible doe was mercy employee notwithstanding any other status the law may or may not have reposed on her for example,a student.we rely on nationwide mutual insurance darden,to decide if a person is an employee under title vii,see covington,at.applied to doe complaint,darden factors indeed suggest she was an employee under title vii.see at.
for instance,mercy was the source of the instrumentalities and tools of doe work as a resident,the location of doe work was at mercy,and mercy assigned doe projects and tasks.see id.doe had no discretion over when and how long she worked beyond acgme guidelines limiting her workweek to hours.see id.and assuming she was paid a plausible assumption,we think,her paychecks were taxed like other employees under the federal insurance contributions act,or fica.see see mayo,at.she had no apparent role in hiring or paying assistants,her work was part of mercy regular business of providing healthcare to patients,and she could bargain collectively as a resident like other employees.see darden,at boston med.ctr.in sum,we agree with mercy that,had doe complied with title vii administrative requirements,she could have filed title vii claims in court as an employee like other residents have before.see,takele mayo clinic,cir.
nevertheless we reject the rest of mercy argument.title vii concurrent applicability does not bar doe private causes of action for retaliation and quid pro quo harassment under title ix.six supreme court decisions guide us.
first is johnson railway express agency,which involved whether the timely filing of an eeoc charge alleging race discrimination under title vii tolled the limitations period on a claim alleging race discrimination under,a statute without administrative requirements.though it ultimately found the latter claim untimely,the court held that the remedies available under title vii and under,although related,and although directed to most of the same ends,are separate,distinct,and independent.id.at.despite title vii range and design as a comprehensive solution for invidious discrimination in employment,the court explained,a employee clearly is not deprived of other remedies and is limited to title vii in his search for relief.id.at.title vii manifests a congressional intent to allow an individual to pursue independently his rights under both title vii and other applicable federal statutes.id.quoting alexander.the employer argued that allowing johnson claim to proceed might permit his circumvention of title vii administrative requirements,going against congress intent.but the court disagreed 
conciliation and persuasion through the eeoc administrative process under title vii,to be sure,often constitute a desirable approach to settlement of disputes based on sensitive and emotional charges of invidious employment discrimination.we recognize,too,that the filing of a lawsuit under might tend to deter efforts at conciliation,that lack of success in the legal action could weaken the eeoc efforts to induce voluntary compliance,and that a suit is privately oriented and narrow,rather than broad,in application,as successful conciliation tends to be.but these are the natural effects of the choice congress has made available to the claimant by its conferring upon him independent administrative and judicial remedies.the choice is a valuable one.under some circumstances,the administrative route may be highly preferred over the litigatory under others the reverse may be true.
id.at emphasis added.the court thus declined to infer any positive preference for title vii without a more definite congressional expression.id.
a year later came brown general services administration,which involved an amendment to title vii see that waived sovereign immunity to grant federal employees access to administrative and judicial relief from workplace discrimination.alleging race discrimination,an employee filed claims under and,but the latter was untimely under the amendment jurisdictional limitations period.holding that congress intended as the exclusive,administrative and judicial scheme for the redress of federal employment discrimination,at emphasis added,the supreme court affirmed dismissal of brown case for want of jurisdiction.critically,the court distinguished johnson as inapposite,for johnson held only that title vii does other remedies in private employment,not federal employment.id.at.johnson inapplicability was especially plain,the court found,because private employment does raise problems of sovereign immunity.id.
then in,seven years after title ix enactment,the court decided cannon,in which an applicant sued a medical school alleging it denied her admission based on her sex,in violation of title ix.the seventh circuit affirmed dismissal of her claim,holding that congress intended title ix administrative device as the exclusive means to enforce the statute.at.the supreme court disagreed.reading a,it inferred a private cause of action for the applicant to allege the medical school rejected her based on sex,id.at,notwithstanding that title ix does expressly authorize private action,id.at.title ix explicitly confers a benefit on persons discriminated against based on sex,the court held,and the plaintiff was clearly a member of that class for whose special benefit the statute was enacted.id.at emphasis added.
three years later came north haven,in which employees filed title ix agency actions alleging sex discrimination against two school boards.agencies had promulgated regulations interpreting title ix to extend to employment discrimination.see id.at citing,for example,a.the boards sued the agencies,seeking to declare these regulations ultra vires under title ix.voting six to three,the supreme court upheld them,as the agencies had fairly read a broad directive that no person may be discriminated against based on sex to encompass employees as well as students.id.at emphasis added.the court rejected the argument that title ix should extend to private employment because employees have remedies other than those available under title ix,like title vii.id.at.even if alternative remedies are available and their existence is relevant,it rejoined,congress has provided a variety of remedies,at times overlapping,to eradicate employment discrimination.id.citing,among other decisions,johnson,at.
joined by chief justice burger and justice rehnquist,justice powell dissented.given title vii comprehensive scheme and carefully prescribed procedures for eeoc conciliation,he would have held that title ix does extend to private employment,as title ix has no time limits for action,no conciliation provisions,and no guidance as to procedure.id.at powell,dissenting.he also thought it unlikely congress would duplicate enforcement of titles vii and ix in employment by different departments of government with different enforcement powers,areas of expertise,and enforcement methods.id.at.
a decade later the court decided franklin gwinnett county public schools,in which a student sought damages for sexual harassment under title ix.acknowledging cannon implied cause of action and relying on the presumption that all appropriate remedies are available for private litigants,id.at,the court held that damages are available in private title ix actions,id.at.
finally in the court decided jackson birmingham board of education,in which a school board relieved a high school employee of his coaching position after he complained that the girls basketball team received unequal treatment based on sex.id.at.he sued in his private capacity,bringing a title ix retaliation claim.reversing the eleventh circuit,the court allowed the employee retaliation claim to proceed under cannon.id.at.if funding recipients were permitted to retaliate freely,the court held,individuals who witness sex discrimination would be loath to report it and all manner of title ix violations might go unremedied.id.at.
