the labor provides that the labor conditions set by the industrial welfare commission commission shall be the standard labor conditions for employees.a commission wage order provides that employees shall be provided suitable seating,if reasonable,during the performance of their duties.the labor code also establishes the private attorneys general act of et seq,which allows an employee to bring an action for civil penalties for violations of provisions of the labor code,except those provisions for which a civil penalty is provided,subds.f,g.
the question in this case is whether an employee may state a cause of action for civil penalties under the private attorneys general act of for violation of the suitable seating order of the commission.we answer that question in the affirmative.
in this action for penalties for violation of the labor code,plaintiff and appellant eugina bright appeals from a judgment of dismissal entered after the trial court sustained the demurrer of defendant and respondent cents only stores to bright class action complaint.bright sought civil penalties under section,subdivision f,for violation of section in that cents only stores failed to provide its employees with suitable seating under commission wage order,subdivision,tit,subd.wage order,subdivision or the suitable seating requirement 
on appeal,bright contends the trial court erred in ruling that violations of wage order,subdivision are not violations of section and civil penalties under section,subdivision f are not available,because commission wage order has its own penalty provision.we conclude violations of wage order,subdivision are violations of section and civil penalties under section,subdivision f are available.accordingly,we reverse the judgment.
procedural background 
allegations of complaint 
in class action complaint for violation of the labor code private attorneys general act of et,filed june,bright alleged she was employed as a cashier at cents only stores.the cents only stores did not provide its cashiers with seats,despite the fact that the nature of the work reasonably permitted the use of seats.in the complaint sole cause of action,bright alleged cents only stores violated section by failing to provide with a seat in violation of wage order,subdivision.bright alleged she satisfied all conditions for filing the complaint,including exhaustion of administrative remedies.bright sought penalties against cents only stores under section,subdivision f,attorney fees,and costs.
demurrer to the complaint 
the cents only stores contended the complaint failed to allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action because a violation of wage order,subdivision is not a violation of section and even if violations of wage order,subdivision are unlawful under section,civil penalties are not available under section,subdivision f,because commission wage order has its own penalty provision.
trial court ruling 
the trial court sustained the demurrer without leave to amend,dismissed the complaint with prejudice,and entered judgment in favor of cents only stores.the court ruled a failure to provide suitable seating is not a violation of section,because such failure is not a condition prohibited by wage order,subdivision.even if a failure to provide suitable seating is a prohibited condition of labor,civil penalties are not recoverable under section,subdivision f,because commission wage order contains its own civil penalty provision in subdivision subdivision,which restricts civil penalties to cases where the employee was underpaid.because bright can not allege she was underpaid,cents only stores demurrer was well taken.

standard of review 
in reviewing the sufficiency of a complaint against a general demurrer,we are guided by rules.we treat the demurrer as admitting all material facts properly pleaded,but not contentions,deductions or conclusions of fact or law.citation.we also consider matters which may be judicially noticed.citation.further,we give the complaint a reasonable interpretation,reading it as a whole and its parts in their context.citation.when a demurrer is sustained,we determine whether the complaint states facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.citation.and when it is sustained without leave to amend,we decide whether there is a reasonable possibility that the defect can be cured by amendment if it can be,the trial court has abused its discretion and we reverse if not,there has been no abuse of discretion and we affirm.citations.the burden of proving such reasonable possibility is squarely on the plaintiff.blank kirwan cal d.
suitable seating is a condition of labor encompassed by section 
bright contends section encompasses violations of wage order,subdivision,such that a violation of the latter is a violation of the former.neither party cites any appellate authority interpreting section in the context of the suitable seating provision,and none has been discovered in our independent research.we conclude that bright arguments are correct.

section plain meaning controls our interpretation.the suitable seating requirement is a standard condition of labor fixed by the commission.such conditions shall be the standard conditions of labor for employees.ibid.by incorporating the suitable seating requirement into section required conditions of labor,a violation of the suitable seating requirement is a violation of section.employment under a condition that violates section is employment under conditions of labor prohibited by the order and,accordingly,is unlawful.
